Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a Notary Public, and what do they do?

    A Notary Public is a certified official appointed by the government to witness the signing of documents and certify their authenticity.

  • What types of documents can a Notary Public notarize?

    Notaries can notarize various documents, including affidavits, contracts, deeds, powers of attorney, and loan documents.

  • What is the process for notarizing a document?

    The signer must appear in person before the Notary Public, provide valid identification, and sign the document in their presence.

  • Can a Notary Public provide legal advice?

    No, Notaries cannot offer legal advice. Their role is to verify signatures and ensure proper execution of documents.

  • Can motary notary provide legal advice?

    Yes, we do provide legal assistance.

  • What is an acknowledgment?

    An acknowledgment is a formal declaration by the signer that they willingly signed the document.

  • What is a jurat?

    A jurat is a notarial act where the signer swears or affirms the truthfulness of the contents of the document.

  • How much does notarization cost?
